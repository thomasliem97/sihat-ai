SihatAI is an end-to-end multimodal clinical intelligence platform for Malaysia’s healthcare ecosystem. It addresses rising diagnostic demand, specialist shortages, fragmented records, delayed interpretation, language barriers, and patients’ difficulty understanding complex medical information. The platform turns medical images, laboratory and clinical documents, historical records, and spoken symptoms into structured, evidence-grounded insights for physicians and patients in English, Bahasa Melayu, Mandarin, and Tamil.

Its AI engine is powered by MedGemma 1.5 with MY-LoRA, a shipped Malaysia-focused QLoRA adapter loaded at serve time through PEFT. The adapter incorporates Malaysian clinical terminology, reporting conventions, and dual-audience communication style without retraining the full foundation model. Production inference runs live by default through FastAPI and Modal. Supported modalities include X-rays, CT and MRI montages, dermatology and ophthalmology images, histopathology patch montages, laboratory PDFs, and clinical documents.

Every submission enters a seven-stage agentic pipeline: upload, sibling PII de-identification, modality detection and routing, MedGemma with MY-LoRA analysis (including imaging and document specialist hops), hybrid evidence retrieval, ALLOW/WARN clinical guardrails, and report composition. The orchestrator extracts structured findings and biomarkers, flags abnormal or critical values, localizes visual findings with prompted bounding boxes, applies heuristic confidence gating, analyses biomarker trends, and compares results with prior studies. Critical cases write durable audit events and notify the clinician.

To reduce hallucination, SihatAI uses hybrid Retrieval-Augmented Generation over Malaysian Ministry of Health Clinical Practice Guidelines: dense retrieval fused with BM25, then maximal marginal relevance. Findings and recommendations include traceable sources, guideline sections, excerpts, and relevance scores. Weak retrieval returns empty citations rather than static stubs. The safety layer applies low-confidence abstention, WARN veto of patient prose, critical-value escalation, medical disclaimers, physician sign-gated patient release, and human-review routing. Role-based authorization, policies, HMAC-signed webhooks, and controlled record access protect sensitive health data.

One findings object generates two audience-specific reports. Physicians receive technical summaries, confidence-scored differentials, visual annotations, longitudinal comparisons, recommendations, and citations, then edit and sign the draft. Patients receive plain-language explanations, warning signs, action plans, and questions for their doctor in their preferred language, only when policy allows and the physician has signed. Voice triage uses MedASR with Whisper fallback, plus minimal browser text-to-speech prompts, to convert spoken symptoms into structured complaints and routine, urgent, or emergency classifications.

The stack is decoupled and scalable: Python, FastAPI, Pydantic, Uvicorn, and HTTPX power the AI microservice; PHP 8.4 and Laravel 13 manage records, queues, webhooks, policies, and Fortify authentication; Vue 3, TypeScript, Inertia.js 3, Wayfinder, Tailwind CSS 4, shadcn-vue, Reka UI, and Vite deliver the physician and patient apps. Quality is backed by Pest 4, Larastan, TypeScript, ESLint, Prettier, factories, seed data, and an eval regression gate, with a dashboard tracking reasoning, accuracy, citation grounding, confidence, report quality, and safety, and demo_seed runs watermarked apart from live suites.

SihatAI is a localized, explainable, safety-engineered clinical workflow. It helps clinicians review faster, keeps AI outputs auditable, and gives Malaysians clearer access to their health information without replacing professional medical judgment.
