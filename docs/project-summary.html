01

Platform overview

SihatAI is an end-to-end multimodal clinical intelligence platform built for Malaysia's healthcare ecosystem. It addresses rising diagnostic demand, specialist shortages, fragmented records, delayed interpretation, language barriers, and patients' difficulty understanding complex medical information. The platform transforms medical images, laboratory and clinical documents, historical records, and spoken symptoms into structured, evidence-grounded insights for physicians and patients in English, Bahasa Melayu, Mandarin, and Tamil.

Its AI engine is powered by MedGemma 1.5 with MY-LoRA, a shipped Malaysia-focused QLoRA adapter loaded at serve time through PEFT. The adapter incorporates Malaysian clinical terminology, reporting conventions, and dual-audience communication style without retraining the complete foundation model. Production inference runs live by default through FastAPI and Modal. The multimodal architecture supports X-rays, CT and MRI montages, dermatology and ophthalmology images, histopathology patch montages, laboratory PDFs, and clinical documents through one unified interface.

02

Pipeline and grounding

Every submission enters a seven-stage agentic pipeline: upload, sibling PII de-identification (safe file path), modality detection and routing, MedGemma with MY-LoRA analysis including imaging and document specialist hops, hybrid evidence retrieval, ALLOW/WARN clinical guardrails, and report composition. The orchestrator extracts structured findings and biomarkers, identifies abnormal or critical values, localizes visual findings with prompted bounding boxes, applies heuristic confidence gating, analyses biomarker trends, and compares current results with previous studies. Critical cases write durable audit events and notify the clinician. Machine-readable outputs support dashboards, trend visualization, reproducible evaluation, and future hospital-system interoperability.

To reduce hallucination, SihatAI uses hybrid Retrieval-Augmented Generation over Malaysian Ministry of Health Clinical Practice Guidelines: dense retrieval fused with BM25, then maximal marginal relevance. Findings and recommendations include traceable sources, guideline sections, excerpts, and relevance scores. Weak retrieval returns empty citations rather than static stubs. The safety layer applies low-confidence abstention, WARN veto of patient prose, critical-value escalation, medical disclaimers, physician sign-gated patient release, and human-review routing. Role-based authorization, policies, HMAC-signed webhooks, and controlled record access protect sensitive health data.

03

Dual audiences and voice

One findings object generates two audience-specific reports. Physicians receive technical summaries, confidence-scored differential diagnoses, visual annotations, longitudinal comparisons, recommendations, and supporting citations, then edit and sign the draft. Patients receive plain-language explanations, warning signs, practical action plans, and questions for their doctor in their preferred language, only when policy allows and the physician has signed. Voice triage uses MedASR with Whisper fallback, plus minimal browser text-to-speech interview prompts, to convert spoken symptoms into structured complaints, follow-up questions, and routine, urgent, or emergency classifications.

04

Architecture and quality

SihatAI uses a decoupled, scalable architecture. Python, FastAPI, Pydantic, Uvicorn, and HTTPX power the AI inference microservice. PHP 8.4 and Laravel 13 manage medical records, relational data, database queues, background jobs, webhook callbacks, authorization policies, and Fortify authentication. Vue 3, TypeScript, Inertia.js 3, Laravel Wayfinder, Tailwind CSS 4, shadcn-vue, Reka UI, and Vite deliver responsive physician and patient applications with typed navigation and reusable clinical components.

Engineering quality is supported by Pest 4 automated tests, Larastan static analysis, TypeScript validation, ESLint, Prettier, database factories, reproducible seed data, and an eval regression gate. A dedicated evaluation dashboard tracks medical reasoning, report accuracy, citation grounding, confidence presentation, report quality, and safety compliance, with demo_seed runs clearly watermarked apart from live suites.

SihatAI is a complete, localized, explainable, and safety-engineered clinical workflow. It helps clinicians review information faster, makes AI outputs auditable, and gives Malaysians clearer access to their health information without attempting to replace professional medical judgment.
